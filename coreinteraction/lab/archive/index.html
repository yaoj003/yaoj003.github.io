COURSE DESCRIPTION

This course serves as a complement to Core Studio Interaction. The assignments are build to work in tandem with the projects students are developing in the studio class. The lab is designed around a series of small work-shops that teach beginning and intermediate interaction design through a hands on engagement with HTML and CSS.

BOARDS

I was inspired to do a pie chart to represent my sections by this website. I loved the fun and quirky colors, which made the website bright and approachable. I realized that while the colors and the design were being used as only decoration here, I could used to them define each section.

Studio LoveLock Homepage



I researched the Swiss graphic designer Walter Herdeg, specifically his work on data visualization in his book Graphis Diagrams. Herdeg is known for his quintessential Swiss design and has been called the “epitome of a post-war Modernist.” One of his greatest contributions was the creation and publication of Graphis, a bi-monthly design magazine.

Here are some pages from Graphis Diagrams.



A designer and programmer named Rune Madsen was inspired by Herdeg’s work and created an interactive website using Processing that displays a diagram of words from nine famous speeches. While this is beyond what I am trying to make, it was really interesting to see a diagram as virtual and interactive thing.

SKETCHES

WEEK 1
For the first week of this project, I brainstormed ideas about how to represent a syllabus. I wanted my webpage to stay true to the form of an archive and to be easy to navigate. Since our syllabus is structured into sections, I wanted each section to be separate. I explored forms of data visualization, icons, and hanging mobiles for the structure of this webpage.

2015: The Year in Visual Stories and Graphics
Icons for Everyday Use
Hanging Mobiles by Alexander Calder

WEEK 2
I decided to draw inspiration for my webpage from interactive graphs. This week, I sketched out the webpage using a pie chart to represent the size of each section based on the length of the content and its importance in our class curriculum.

WEEK 3
After this week's class, I revised my idea of the pie chart to be more minimalistic and easier to make. Here, I wanted to use the vertical bars to represent the "size" of each section.





In the end, I made the section bars horizontal and different lengths to represent the different "sizes." This was a lot easier than making the bars vertical and allowed me to use the scroll function inside the section contents. I used jQuery to make the accordion interface, which Ryan suggested I research last week.

PROJECTS

  • ARCHIVAL INTERFACE DESIGN (3 WEEKS)
    Design (wireframe and compose) individual index page. Page will exist as an archivefor s emesters syllabus content, information, coursework, readings, sketches etc.

  • DESIGN RESEARCH AND ANALYSIS (15 WEEKS)
    Design and compose a response to a designer or text. Responses should take into consideration the character and tenants of the original work(s); typographic, written, color, concept, layout etc. and should be informative as well as beautiful. Responses
    will be shared in presentation format and collected into a design historical catalog that the students will collaboratively design.
